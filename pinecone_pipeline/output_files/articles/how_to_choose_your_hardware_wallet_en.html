How can you know which crypto wallet is best for you? Not all hardware wallets are created equal. Understanding how they work and what they are made of will guide you to choose the perfect device for your assets.

Why Do I Need a Hardware Wallet?

As you’ll know, if you’re buying any sort of crypto, a hardware wallet is non-negotiable. There are a couple of reasons for that. Firstly, as you know, it keeps your most important data offline. This means your wallet is safe from online attacks. Secondly it also gives you full ownership and control over your cryptocurrencies by giving you sole access to your private keys . This is something a software wallet cannot offer by design.

In short, hardware wallets are devices that ensure your private keys are stored offline. Unlike digital or hot wallets, they provide a layer of security against the evolving threats that come from being connected to the internet.

Yet, even among the hardware wallets family, not all devices are equal. Like computers or mobiles, they are not all worth the same.

But what makes them so different?

Well, mainly, their pull is due to their secure components.

Why Are Hardware Wallets Secure?

To explain, the security level of a hardware wallet depends on the quality of protection layers.

Remember, cryptocurrencies are assets that need specific security protection. In the same way that you wouldn’t build a vault out of lego bricks, you wouldn’t build a hardware wallet using components taken from your coffee machine. Or would you?

Components of a Hardware Wallet

To clarify, hardware wallets typically consist of three primary security features or layers:

  • A hardware (physical) component. Like how computers and mobiles work with a processor, hardware wallets use a chip.
  • An operating system, like Microsoft Windows for PC, macOS for Apple computers or Android for most smartphones.
  • An anti-tampering authentication check. This checking process can include several steps, you often have both a PIN code and a password to completely unlock your smartphone. It can include up to 2 steps for the most secure hardware wallets.

Naturally, the security of these features varies from wallet to wallet. That’s right – not all hardware wallets use the same components and not all of them work as effectively as each other to keep your funds secure.

Therefore, “tailor-made” operating systems can offer much more flexibility to support crypto assets.
