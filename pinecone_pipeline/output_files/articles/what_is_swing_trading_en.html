What Is Swing Trading?

Swing trading is a trading technique used to cash in on short-term to medium-term price volatility in the cryptocurrency market. Traders buy in and hold their positions typically from a couple of days to a few weeks. There are also swing trades where traders hold their positions for a couple of months.

This technique revolves around the revered ‘volatility’ of the crypto market. This can be courtesy of a plethora of factors ranging from a simple tweet to blanket bans.

Swing traders aim to identify price movements in advance based on multiple macro and microeconomic factors. They then buy in and wait for the market volatility to play out in their favor.

Day Trading vs. Swing Trading: What’s The Difference?

Day trading refers to placing multiple trades in a single day to capitalize on the same-day price actions. That means traders enter and exit the day trades on the same day. Generally speaking, day traders make money from the number of winning trades rather than the margin of wins.

Contrarily, swing trading is a more long-term approach to generating sizable returns from a price swing that can take weeks or months to play out.

Crypto Swing Trading Strategies: How Traders Work?

There are four common strategies that crypto swing traders employ to execute their trades. Let’s check them out.

Riding Market Trends

This is a simple strategy that involves placing trades in the direction of the existing trends in the market. Essentially, swing traders will analyze trading volume to determine the strength of a trend. This dictates the price action potential and the timeframe needed to turn a profit.

Buy the Dip

Buying the pullback, or ‘buying the dip’ is a strategy that involves entering a trade after a temporary price decline within an established price movement direction. Put simply, if an asset is still increasing in value over a longer period of time, a swing trader will buy in when the price drops marginally. This helps traders maximize profitability in a prevailing trend.

Mirror the Market

As part of this strategy, traders try to follow where the money is in the market. This means they spot what assets short-term investors are trying to bet their money on to make profits. It’s easy to spot these instances in the money flowing in and out of altcoins.

Market Reversal Trading

This is a trading strategy that relies on trend reversals after an extended period of price movement in a single direction. One of the most common ways traders spot a trend reversal is when an asset’s candlestick price chart forms a Doji pattern .

Best Tools for Crypto Swing Traders

Swing traders employ a range of crypto trading tools to plan and strategize their trade setups. Apart from finding a reliable and safe crypto exchange to trade on, here are the tools that traders use.

Charting Tools

Trading charts represent the price movement of crypto assets over a period of time. Thus, charting tools are the  most basic requirement for swing trading.

There are many types of trading charts, ranging from line and bar charts to candlestick charts. For complex price analysis, however, most traders prefer to use candlestick charts.

Technical Indicators

Beyond basic chart essentials, traders also use a wide range of technical indicators to analyze price movements and set up trades. These indicators act as visual representations of various aspects that affect the price movement of a crypto asset.

For instance, the support and resistance indicator shows the price range at which the buying and selling pressure is high. Then, an accumulation and distribution line indicates the volume of money flowing in and out of a cryptocurrency.

Market Data

Swing traders rely on both micro and macroeconomic factors to place their trades. Hence, alongside technical data, swing traders also use market data to build their strategies. This aggregates all relevant information like tokenomics, market capitalization , and trading volume of a crypto asset along with key industry news, policy developments, and more.

These 3 tools are prerequisites for any swing trader. Apart from the tools, it is also imperative for traders to know the risks of swing trading too.

Risks of Crypto Swing Trading

Nobody ever said trading was easy. The volatility of crypto market can be overwhelming, and there are some risks involved with swing trading. . Here are some risks you should be aware of:

Timing the market is risky: Crypto markets are infamous for major fluctuations within hours or even minutes. This may turn a winning trade into a loss-making one in no time. Besides, timing the market comes at the cost of having to be in front of the charts at all times.

Market manipulation: Artificial money movements and coordinated pump and dumps can take a significant toll on a swing trader’s capital. Similarly, large capital players or whales can completely disorganize the market price of a small altcoin with a few trades.

Opportunity cost: Active trading runs a cost of avoiding long-term gains in favor of shorter price movements. Also, actively swing trading crypto requires holistic knowledge and experience in both technical and fundamental analysis.

You may, however, mitigate these risks by following some basic precautions such as; setting a stop-loss to limit losses, journaling trades to reflect on trading performance and avoiding emotional trades.
