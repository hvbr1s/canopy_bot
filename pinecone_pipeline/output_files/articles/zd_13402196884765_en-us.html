Receiving Airdrops as a Ledger User

Airdrops have gained popularity as a way to distribute new tokens or coins to existing holders of a specific cryptocurrency. If you own that particular cryptocurrency, like many Ledger users do, you may receive free tokens from a newly launched project. Ledger users are especially well-positioned for airdrops because Ledger activity is all on-chain, and you have a real crypto address that you own. Compare this to centralized exchange (CEX) users who don’t control an address of their own, and most activity happens only within the CEX platform and not on-chain. The ownership and on-chain activity that the Ledger ecosystem provides increases the likelihood of being eligible for and receiving airdrops.

However, it's essential to be cautious because not all airdrops can be trusted, especially when their origins are unclear. This article provides guidance on how to safely participate in airdrops and offers tips for maintaining security in airdrop situations, where scams are common.

Can I receive airdrops as a Ledger user? 

Absolutely, as a Ledger user, you can receive airdrops. Your on-chain transaction history and control over your crypto addresses provide you with opportunities to participate in different airdrop campaigns. To claim an airdrop, you will generally want to follow the instructions provided by the official airdrop website. You may need to connect to the website via WalletConnect, or use a third-party wallet to orchestrate a claim. For high-profile airdrops, Ledger support will often post a guide, so definitely search our Help Center if you feel like you need help. If in doubt, Ledger support is happy to help answer your questions.

Nevertheless, when engaging in airdrop activities, it is imperative to exercise vigilance and verify the authenticity of these opportunities to steer clear of potential scams.

Airdrop Scams

Airdrops can serve as a breeding ground for scams. Here are a few examples of the types of airdrop scams you might encounter:

Fake Airdrop Websites
Scammers create fraudulent websites that mimic legitimate airdrop campaigns, enticing users to provide their private keys, wallet addresses, or personal information.
Malicious Downloads
Some scams involve encouraging users to download a fake airdrop application or wallet software, which may contain malware designed to steal cryptocurrency holdings.
Phishing Emails and Messages
Scammers send phishing emails or messages claiming to be from airdrop organizers. These messages often contain links to fake websites or request sensitive information.
Impersonating Well-Known Projects
Scammers pretend to represent established cryptocurrency projects and announce airdrops. Users may be tricked into thinking they're participating in a legitimate event.
Social Media Scams
Fraudsters create fake social media profiles or impersonate influencers to promote fake airdrops. Users are directed to phishing websites or asked to send cryptocurrency to participate.
Unsolicited Airdrops and NFT Scams
Users may receive unsolicited airdrops in their wallets that claim to be valuable or related to NFTs. However, these airdrops often come with accompanying links that lead to phishing sites, malicious downloads, or requests for sensitive information. 
Telegram and Discord Scams
Fraudulent airdrop announcements are made on messaging platforms like Telegram or Discord. Users are lured into participating but end up revealing private keys or personal data.
Smart Contract Exploitation
Scammers create fake airdrop smart contracts that deceive users into sending cryptocurrency to an address controlled by the scammer. Take a look at our detailed guide on revoking smart contract allowances here. 
Bait-and-Switch Airdrops
Users are promised valuable airdrops, but after participating, they receive worthless tokens or nothing at all.
Voucher Scams
Scammers distribute NFT airdrop vouchers that promise rewards but lead to malicious websites or actions that compromise security.

What can I do to protect myself from airdrop scams?

  • Verify airdrop sources: Participate only in airdrops from well-known and trusted projects. Verify legitimacy through official sources.
  • Double-check URLs: Ensure you're on the correct website when signing up for an airdrop. Beware of fake sites with similar URLs.
  • Never share your 24-word secret recovery phrase: Protect your Ledger device's private keys and 24-word recovery phrase. Legitimate airdrops do not require this information.
  • Use official channels: Interact with airdrop projects through their official websites or social media profiles. Avoid unsolicited messages or emails.
  • Ignore unsolicited airdrops and NFTs: Be cautious of unsolicited airdrops in your wallet. Research the source and token before taking action. Learn more about how to handle unknown NFTs here.
  • Educate yourself: Stay informed about common cryptocurrency scams and phishing techniques to recognize and avoid them.
  • Report suspicious activity: Report any suspicious airdrop or scam encounters to relevant authorities and share information with the community to help others stay safe.
