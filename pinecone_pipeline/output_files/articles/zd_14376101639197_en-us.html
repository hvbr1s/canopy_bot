How do I manage my assets if my device fails?

When your Ledger device fails or isn’t working properly, it can be a stressful situation, especially if you need to manage your assets urgently. This article will guide you on what to do in such situations and provide a step-by-step solution to access your funds using your Secret Recovery Phrase (SRP) on a different wallet, while also explaining the associated risks and precautions you should take.

Important Safety Reminder

We never recommend entering your Secret Recovery Phrase (SRP) into an online wallet. However, please note that SRPs are generic, so technically, you can input them into any wallet that follows the BIP39 standard if you find yourself in a difficult situation.

Step One: Stay Calm and Troubleshoot

Remember that your assets are secure as long as you have your SRP. This phrase is crucial for accessing your funds. Before you consider other alternatives, please take the following troubleshooting steps:

  • Try using a different USB cable to connect your Ledger device. Check out our article about USB connection issues for more information. 
  • Make sure your Ledger Live application is updated to the latest version.
  • Ensure your device's firmware is updated by following our guides for the Ledger for Ledger Nano S, Ledger Nano S Plus, and Ledger Nano X.

If you have any doubts or are unsure about these steps, please don't hesitate to reach out to Ledger Support for assistance.

Step Two: Understand Your Options

In the event that your Ledger device is not operational, you have two main options:

  1. Purchase a new Ledger device and use your SRP to restore and access your assets. This is the recommended course of action. Then, restore your device by entering your existing SRP.

     If you're in the US and require a device urgently, consider checking local Best Buy stores.

  2. Import your SRP into a third-party wallet that supports the BIP-39 Standard to gain access to your assets.

While this article will discuss the second option in more detail, it's essential to note that we always recommend using a Ledger device for maximum security.

Step 3: Using a Third-Party Wallet

If you opt to use a third-party wallet, please follow these steps:

  1. Choose a third-party wallet that you trust and that supports the cryptocurrencies you want to manage.

  2. Download and install the wallet, ensuring to double-check the website's authenticity and the correct download links to prevent phishing scams.

  3. Select the option to import a wallet using a recovery phrase, then enter your SRP to access your assets.

  4. After you've accessed and managed your assets, consider transferring them to a secure wallet, ideally back to a Ledger device, as soon as possible.

Risks and Precautions

It's important to be aware of the risks associated with using third-party wallets. Here are some vital precautions you should take:

  1. Always opt for wallets from reputable providers.

  2. Double-check the wallet’s website and download links to prevent falling victim to phishing scams.

  3. Note that some wallets may not support all the cryptocurrencies you have stored in your Ledger wallet.

Conclusion

Using a third-party wallet can be a convenient solution when your Ledger device is not functioning. However, it's imperative to be aware of the potential risks and to take all necessary precautions to safeguard your assets. Remember, the safest and most recommended option for managing your cryptocurrency is always through a Ledger device.
