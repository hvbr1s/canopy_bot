Where is my ETH/ERC20 deposit from Binance (BNB)?

If you've sent ETH/ERC20 deposits to your Ledger deposit address and they appear to be missing from your ETH account, there's no need to worry. With the simple steps of installing the Binance Smart Chain (BNB) app and adding an account, you can swiftly locate and regain access to your ETH/ERC20 tokens on the BNB network. This article will walk you through the process of locating your ETH/ERC20 tokens in Ledger Live and provide guidance on swapping them back to the ETH Network.

ETH/ERC20 Tokens Missing in Ledger Live

Ledger Live now fully supports the Binance Smart Chain (BNB) network, enabling easy access to your ETH funds when sent via BNB instead of the Ethereum network.

Option 1: Create a Binance Smart Chain (BNB) account in Ledger Live (Recommended) 
  1. Open Ledger Live and navigate to the My Ledger tab.
  2. Connect and unlock your Ledger device.
  3. If asked, allow My Ledger to access your device.
  4. Search for the Binance Smart Chain (BNB) app in the app catalog.
  5. Click the Install button to install the app on your Ledger device.
  6. Select Add Account. 
  7. Navigate to the Accounts tab.
  8. Click on your Binance Smart Chain account, and you'll find your ETH/ERC20 tokens listed below.
Option 2: Use Zerion in the Discover section

If you can't see your coins, consider using Zerion as an alternative method to locate your tokens.

Option 3: Use MetaMask with your Ledger device to locate your Ledger BNB account
  1. Download and install the MetaMask extension on your computer browser.
  2. Connect your Ledger wallet to MetaMask by following these instructions.
  3. Open MetaMask in an expanded view and connect your MetaMask to BNB network using this tutorial.

You should now be able to see your ETH/ERC20 on the BNB network in your MetaMask Ledger account.

  Withdrawing funds from an exchange? Make sure to select the correct network!

When transferring crypto from an exchange to your Ledger wallet, ensure that the withdrawal network chosen on the exchange matches your account's network. Selecting the incorrect network could lead to your crypto assets not being visible in your Ledger Live accounts. Learn more

Converting Your ETH from the BNB Network to the ETH Network

1) Using the CIC App (Availability Varies by Country)

You can use the CIC app to swap your ETH or ERC20 tokens from the BNB network to the Ethereum network. Please note that CIC availability may vary by country.

2) Using an Exchange (Binance or Coinbase)

If CIC is not available in your country, you can follow these steps to swap to the Ethereum Network:

Sending BNB-based ETH to an Exchange:

  1. Access your exchange account.

  2. Navigate to the deposit/receive section.

  3. Select ETH or the specific token (BEP20 format).

  4. Copy the BNB deposit address provided by the exchange.

  5. Open Ledger Live, then send your ETH/ERC20 tokens on the BNB network by pasting your exchange's wallet address. For detailed instructions on sending funds within Ledger Live, consult our step-by-step guide.

Swapping on the Exchange:

  1. Wait for the ETH to arrive at the exchange.

  2. Select the trading pair, ensuring you swap your BNB-based ETH for ETH on the Ethereum Network.

  3. Proceed to place the trade for the assets you desire.

Sending Funds Back to Ledger:

  1. Click Receive in the left-hand menu in Ledger Live.

  2. Select the account and click Continue.

  3. Connect and unlock your Ledger device, then click Continue.

  4. On your device, verify the displayed address matches the one in Ledger Live. If they match, approve it on your device.

  5. Copy the address from Ledger Live and Paste it into the exchange. Always verify the address matches before confirming the transaction for added security.

If you're experiencing any difficulties with locating your ETH or completing the swap, don't hesitate to contact our customer support team. We're here to assist you.
