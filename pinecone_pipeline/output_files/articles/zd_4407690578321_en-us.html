Solving Error 0x6a83 or 0x6811

This article describes how to solve errors 0x6a83 or 0x6811 affecting Ethereum-related EVM and Bitcoin applications. 

What is error 0x6a83 and 0x6811?

These errors are displayed when the app you’re trying to install has a prerequisite. Bitcoin and Ethereum are prerequisite apps for many other apps in Ledger Live. Removing your Bitcoin or Ethereum app will remove any apps that depend on them. Please keep in mind, apps have no effect on your funds.

The root cause is a dependency error. A dependency error happens when one app (the dependent app) will not install or update unless another app (the parent app) is already installed and updated to the latest version.

For example, error 0x6a83/0x6811 has recently been affecting Songbird (SBG) and Ethereum (ETH) users. You can see an example here where a user ran into the 0x6a83 error while attempting to update the Ethereum (ETH) app to the latest version.

How to solve errors 0x6a83 and 0x6811

  1. Open Ledger Live.
  2. Go to Settings, select the Experimental features tab and activate Developer mode by using the toggle button.
  3. Navigate to the My Ledger tab and connect your Ledger device.
  4. Find the app in the App installed menu and click the bin icon to uninstall the app.
  5. Reconnect your Ledger device to My Ledger, find the Ethereum app in the App installed section, and update the app to the latest version.
    The Ethereum app updates.
  6. Go back to Settings, select the Experimental features tab and turn off Developer mode by using the toggle button.
  7. Navigate back to the My Ledger tab and connect your Ledger device.
  8. Find the app you originally uninstalled and reinstall it.
