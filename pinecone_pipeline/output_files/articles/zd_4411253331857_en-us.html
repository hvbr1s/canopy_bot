Renaming your Ledger Nano X

Your Nano X comes with a default name, such as “Nano X 158B”. The last part of the default name is generated randomly and is different for every device.

We recommend changing the name of your device to easily identify your Nano X if you use multiple devices and to increase your privacy since the device name is visible when you connect to other devices via Bluetooth.

Viewing the device name

You can view the name of your device from the Ledger Live mobile app or directly from your Nano X.

Ledger Live Mobile Ledger Nano X

To view the device name:

  1. Open the Ledger Live app on your smartphone.
  2. Tap the My Ledger tab.
    The Select your device page appears.

You can view the name of the Nano X device that you have paired with your phone.

  1. Turn on and unlock your Nano X.
  2. Hold both buttons for 3 seconds at any time to access the control center.
  3. Press the right button to navigate to the Device name entry.
    The name of your Nano X is displayed below Device name.

If your device name is too long, it may not be displayed entirely. To see the full device name, access the control center, and navigate to Settings > General > Device name. 

Renaming your device

To rename your Nano X: 

  1. Open the Ledger Live app on your smartphone.
  2. Tap the My Ledger tab.
  3. Select the device for which you want to change the name.

  4. If asked, press both buttons on your device to allow My Ledger to access your device.
    The App catalog page appears.
  5. Tap  Edit next to the Nano X name.
    The Rename device page appears.
  6. Delete the default name and enter a new name for your device. Then tap Confirm.
      Note that some special characters can’t be used in the device name.
  7. Turn on and unlock your Nano X.
  8. Press both buttons on your device to Allow device renaming.

You have successfully renamed your Nano X.

Resetting your device name 

To reset your device name to the original default name, you need to reset your device. To learn how to reset your device, refer to the following article: Reset to factory settings.
