Create a Ledger EOS account with third party apps

This article explains how to create a Ledger EOS account with your Ledger device and third-party software. Check the EOS wallet page to learn more. 

Before you start

  • Update Ledger Live to the latest version via this link.
  • Open Ledger Live and connect your Ledger device to My Ledger to ensure that your Ledger device is running the latest firmware version.
  • Ensure that the latest version of the EOS (EOS) app is installed on your Ledger device.

Step-by-step

  1. Install Scatter EOS on your computer. Install packages are available here.
  2. Open Scatter and click Create new Scatter.
  3. Accept the terms and conditions, create and confirm your password then click Continue.
    Your computer displays: Welcome to Scatter!
  4. Select Get Started > Generate secure keys > I promise I save my secret words! then name your account and skip providing your email.
     The secret words provided by Scatter will not secure your Ledger EOS account, they can be safely discarded.
  5. On your Ledger device, open the EOS app.
    Your Ledger displays: Application is ready.
  6. On the Scatter dashboard, select Keys > Create or import a Key > EOSIO > Import from Hardware.
  7. If it's your first EOS account, leave the index value to 0 then click on the EOS... address that show on your screen.

  8. Find your hardware account in the list and copy your public key.
  9. Open a new browser tab and go to bloks.io.
  10. Go to Wallet > Create Account.
  11. Choose a New Account Name of exactly 12-character long, paste your public key from Scatter into the Public key field then continue with Option 1 (sending EOS from the exchange).
  12. Log into your exchange account and send the exact amount of EOS required to activate the account to the Recipient account.

     Important note about activating your EOS account

    • It's very important to send the exact activation amount or as close as possible to the exact amount.
    • This is because any amount above the activation amount might get lost.
    • Besides, don't forget to add the memo to your transaction.
    • You can check whether your account has been activated by looking up your EOS account name on this explorer.
  13. Once the transaction has been confirmed and your account has been activated, go to the Greymass website and download and install the desktop app.
  14. Open Anchor, select Setup an Account, choose and confirm a password then select the EOS blockchain.
  15. After making sure the EOS app is still open on your Ledger device, go to the Settings button and select Enable Ledger Support.
  16. Select Import an existing Account > Load from Ledger, select the accounts you wish to load, and click Import Account(s).

    Your Ledger EOS account appears. You're done!
